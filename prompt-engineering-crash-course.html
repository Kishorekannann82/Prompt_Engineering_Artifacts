Foundation

What is Prompt Engineering?

Before we dive into techniques, let's understand what prompting actually means — and why it's a skill worth learning.

"A prompt is any text you type to an AI. Prompt Engineering is the skill of writing those prompts in a smart way — so the AI gives you useful, accurate, and well-formatted answers."

🍽️

The Restaurant Analogy

Imagine walking into a restaurant and saying "Give me food."
The waiter has no idea what you want. Do you want veg or non-veg? Spicy or mild? Breakfast or dinner?


But if you say: "One masala dosa, less spicy, with extra chutney please" — you get exactly what you want.


The AI is the chef. You are the customer. Your prompt is the order. A good order = a perfect meal. A vague order = a confused chef.

🧠

AI is powerful but literal

AI models are extremely capable, but they respond to exactly what you say. If you're vague, they guess. If you're clear, they deliver.

🎯

The right input = right output

The quality of your prompt directly controls the quality of the output. Better prompt = better answer, every single time.

🚫

What makes a BAD prompt

Too short. Too vague. No context. No format. No audience. Missing the "what", "who", and "how" of your request.

✅

What makes a GOOD prompt

Clear task + audience + format + constraints. The AI should have zero confusion about what you want.

The anatomy of a great prompt

Task — What do you want?

Start with a clear action word. Write, summarise, explain, classify, translate, list, compare...

Context — What's the background?

Give the AI enough information to understand your situation. "I'm a student", "This is for a sales email", "My audience is beginners".

Format — How should it look?

Tell the AI the structure you want. "In 3 bullet points", "As a table", "In under 100 words", "With an introduction and conclusion".

Tone — How should it sound?

Casual, formal, funny, simple, technical, friendly. The AI will match whatever tone you specify.

Why it matters

Same AI, very different results

This is the most important thing to understand. The AI doesn't change — only your prompt does. Look at this:

Bad Prompt
You typed
Tell me about marketing.
LLM Response
Marketing is the process of promoting products or services to consumers. It includes advertising, social media, content marketing, email marketing, SEO, and many other strategies. The goal is to reach target audiences and convert them into customers. Marketing has evolved significantly with the rise of digital platforms...
Good Prompt
You typed
I run a small bakery in Chennai. Write 3 Instagram captions for my fresh bread posts. Tone: warm, local, and appetising. Keep each caption under 60 words.
LLM Response
1. "Hot out of the oven and straight to your heart 🍞 Our fresh loaves are baked every morning — come in before 9 AM for the best pick!"

2. "Nothing beats the smell of warm bread on a Chennai morning. Visit us today and take a loaf home. Pure goodness, zero preservatives."
💡

The Golden Insight

The AI didn't get smarter. You just gave it better instructions. Prompt engineering is not about the AI — it's about you getting better at communicating with it.

Technique 01
🎯

Zero-shot Prompting

Ask the AI directly — with no examples, no demos. Just your task and your context. You trust the AI's training to handle it.

Beginner Friendly
🧑‍💼
Simple Analogy
You walk up to a stranger who's a doctor and ask: "I have a headache — what could cause it?" You gave zero context, zero examples. If they're knowledgeable, they'll give a useful answer. That's zero-shot: just ask, trust the AI's knowledge.

Use it when:

Simple questions Definitions Summaries Translations Quick rewrites Factual lookups
Bad Prompt
You typed
write something about climate
LLM Response
Climate is a broad topic. It can refer to weather patterns, global warming, biodiversity, or oceanography. Could you please clarify what format you need — essay, bullet points, short or long? Also, who is your target audience? I want to make sure I write the right thing for you...
Good Prompt
You typed
Write a 3-sentence explanation of climate change for a 10-year-old child. Use simple words and a relatable example.
LLM Response
Climate change means the Earth is getting warmer because humans burn fuels like petrol and coal, releasing gases that trap heat — like a blanket around the Earth. This causes hotter summers, stronger storms, and rising seas. We can help by using less electricity, recycling, and planting more trees!

💬 Why the good prompt works

  • It sets the exact length (3 sentences) — no guessing for the AI
  • It names the audience (10-year-old) — so the AI picks simple vocabulary
  • It asks for a "relatable example" — giving creative direction
  • The topic is now specific: "climate change", not vague "climate"
Technique 02
📚

Few-shot Prompting

You teach by example. Give the AI 2–5 sample input→output pairs, then ask it to apply the same pattern to a new input.

Most Powerful for Formatting
🏢
Simple Analogy
Think of training a new intern. Instead of writing a 10-page manual, you show them 3 emails you already wrote and say "Write more like these." They pick up your style instantly. That's few-shot — you teach by showing, not explaining.

Use it when:

Custom writing style Sentiment classification Data formatting Consistent outputs Brand voice
Bad Prompt
You typed
Classify these reviews as positive or negative: "The food was cold." "Absolutely loved it!"
LLM Response
The first review appears negative since cold food is undesirable. The second is clearly positive. Though context matters — cold food could be intentional in some cases like salads or ice cream. Would you like detailed reasoning for each?
Good Prompt (Few-shot)
You typed
Review: "Great service!" → Positive Review: "Very rude staff." → Negative Review: "Average, nothing special." → Neutral Now classify: Review: "The food was cold." → Review: "Absolutely loved it!" →
LLM Response
Review: "The food was cold." → Negative
Review: "Absolutely loved it!" → Positive

💬 Why the good prompt works

  • The examples show the exact output format — one clean label, no essays
  • The AI learns the pattern from 3 pairs and applies it exactly
  • You eliminated the AI's tendency to overthink or over-explain
  • Great for batch processing — the AI stays consistent across all inputs
Technique 03
🧠

Chain of Thought (CoT)

Ask the AI to think step-by-step before giving a final answer. This forces logical reasoning and dramatically reduces mistakes on complex tasks.

Best for Complex Problems
📝
Simple Analogy
In school, your maths teacher always said: "Show your working!" If you just write the answer, you might guess wrong. But when you write each step, you catch your own mistakes. Telling the AI "think step by step" does exactly this — it forces careful reasoning.

Use it when:

Maths & calculations Logical puzzles Multi-step decisions Debugging code Planning problems
Bad Prompt
You typed
A train goes 60 km/h for 2.5 hours, then 80 km/h for 1.5 hours. Total distance?
LLM Response
The total distance is 270 km.
Good Prompt (CoT)
You typed
A train goes 60 km/h for 2.5 hours, then 80 km/h for 1.5 hours. Total distance? Think step by step.
LLM Response
Step 1: First segment — Speed = 60 km/h, Time = 2.5 hrs → Distance = 60 × 2.5 = 150 km.

Step 2: Second segment — Speed = 80 km/h, Time = 1.5 hrs → Distance = 80 × 1.5 = 120 km.

Step 3: Total = 150 + 120 = 270 km.

💬 Why the good prompt works

  • "Think step by step" is a magic phrase — it activates the AI's reasoning mode
  • You can now verify each step independently — great for catching errors
  • Even when the final answer is the same, you can trust it more
  • Works for business decisions, not just maths: "Analyse this situation step by step"
PRO TIP 01

Add "Show your reasoning" or "Explain each step" for even more transparency.

PRO TIP 02

For business: "List pros and cons before concluding" triggers the same deep thinking.

PRO TIP 03

Combine with role prompting: "You are an expert. Think step by step."

Technique 04
🎭

Role Prompting

Give the AI a character or professional identity. This shapes its tone, vocabulary, depth of knowledge, and overall approach to your question.

Great for Expertise & Tone
👨‍⚕️
Simple Analogy
Ask your auto driver about chest pain — you get a vague answer. Ask your family doctor the same question — you get a structured, expert, medically accurate response. Same question. Different role. Completely different quality of answer. Role prompting works the same way with AI.

Use it when:

Professional feedback Audience-specific writing Tone control Simulating an expert Creative personas
Bad Prompt
You typed
Give me feedback on my business idea: I want to sell handmade candles online.
LLM Response
That sounds like a great idea! Handmade candles are very popular right now. You could sell them on Etsy, Instagram, or your own website. Make sure to use nice packaging and market yourself on social media. Good luck!
Good Prompt (Role)
You typed
You are a veteran startup mentor with 15 years of e-commerce experience. Give critical, honest feedback on this idea: I want to sell handmade candles online in India.
LLM Response
The candle market is crowded — your differentiation strategy is everything. Before investing, answer: What's your unique angle (eco-friendly, local scents, luxury gifting)? Can you price above ₹400 and still profit after packaging + shipping? Start with 30 units, test on Instagram Reels, validate demand before building a website.

💬 Why the good prompt works

  • The role ("veteran mentor, 15 years e-commerce") sets both expertise and authority
  • "Critical, honest" as a tone instruction prevents generic encouragement
  • You get actionable, specific advice — not cheerleading
  • Adding "in India" grounds the response in your actual market context

Powerful roles you can try:

ROLE IDEAS

"You are a senior software engineer at Google reviewing my code."

ROLE IDEAS

"You are a primary school teacher. Explain this concept to a class of 8-year-olds."

ROLE IDEAS

"You are a sceptical investor. Find every flaw in my business plan."

Technique 05
⚙️

System Prompt

A hidden set of instructions that defines the AI's identity, rules, and behaviour for an entire conversation — set before the first message.

Used in AI Products & Chatbots
📋
Simple Analogy
When a new employee joins a company, they get an employee handbook on Day 1. It tells them their job title, what to say to customers, what topics to avoid, and what tone to use. Every interaction that follows is shaped by that handbook. A system prompt is exactly that — the AI's handbook for your app or conversation.

Use it when:

Building chatbots Setting brand tone Restricting topics Creating AI products Consistent personality
Bad System Prompt
System Prompt
You are a helpful assistant. Answer questions.
LLM says (to: "Where's my order?")
I'd be happy to help with that! To track your order, you'll need to provide your order number. You can usually find this in your confirmation email. Alternatively, if you have an account, you can log in and check the "My Orders" section. Is there anything else I can assist you with today?
Good System Prompt
System Prompt
You are "Riya", a friendly support agent for FreshBox — an online grocery delivery app in India. Only answer questions about orders, delivery, and products. For anything else, say: "I can only help with FreshBox queries!" Keep replies under 3 sentences. Use a warm, casual Indian English tone.
LLM says (to: "Where's my order?")
Hey! Your order is on its way and should reach you by 6 PM today. You can track it live using the link in your confirmation SMS — just tap it anytime. Let me know if you need anything else! 😊

💬 Why the good system prompt works

  • Name + brand ("Riya" from "FreshBox") creates identity and trust
  • Topic restriction ensures the bot stays focused — no random tangents
  • Reply length limit ("under 3 sentences") keeps conversations fast
  • Tone instruction ("warm, casual Indian English") matches the target audience
  • You set these rules once — they apply to every message automatically
Master Tips

The 6 golden rules of prompting

Apply these to every single prompt you write — regardless of which technique you use.

01

Be specific, not vague

Every vague word is a chance for the AI to misunderstand. Replace "write something" with "write a 100-word product description for..."

02

State the format

Tell the AI exactly how the output should look: bullet points, numbered list, table, paragraph, JSON, code, email, etc.

03

Define your audience

"Explain to a 10-year-old" vs "Explain to a PhD student" — same topic, completely different language and depth.

04

Give context

The more background you give, the better the answer. "I'm a freelancer, I have a difficult client who..." beats just "help with client".

05

Set constraints

Word limits, tone, language, what to avoid — constraints are your friends. "Under 50 words, no jargon, friendly tone" is powerful.

06

Iterate and improve

If the first answer isn't great, refine your prompt. Prompting is a dialogue — ask the AI to "make it shorter", "add examples", "change the tone".
